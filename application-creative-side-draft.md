# Creative Side Draft

Prompt:

> Every person has a creative side, and it can be expressed in many ways: problem solving, original and innovative thinking, and artistically, to name a few. Describe how you express your creative side.

Draft:

I play electric guitar, and I like the part where a riff starts out messy but slowly turns into something I want to keep playing. Coding feels similar to me now. I usually begin with a half-formed idea, something I am not even sure will work, and then I keep shaping it until it becomes a real system.

That started when my dad and I began experimenting with language models. At first we were just curious. We wanted to know whether AI could help build something useful, not just another demo. What pulled me in was the back-and-forth. I would ask for a version of an idea, run it, find the bugs, change the logic, and try again. The model could help move fast, but I still had to decide what was wrong, what mattered, and what to test next.

That process became more serious when I got interested in crypto. I took a cryptocurrency class at Irvine Valley College, which gave me the foundation, but I did not really understand decentralized finance until I started building around it. One of my first projects was a trading bot for Solana meme coins. The original goal was small: maybe it could help pay for API costs. Instead, it turned into a much bigger project about market behavior, timing, and how quickly an algorithm can fail when the real world does not match the idea in your head.

As I kept building, I ran into a different problem. I was reading so much about crypto, AI, and trading that my notes became almost useless. I had information everywhere, but I could not retrieve it when I needed it. So I built LLM Wiki, a personal knowledge system that lets me ask questions in plain language and get answers based on what I have actually saved and read.

Lately, I have been working more on agentic AI. I started with small systems that could recover when a bot broke. Now I am exploring agents that can evaluate information and make strategy decisions with less constant input from me.

For me, creativity is not only making something look finished. It is following a question far enough to find out where it breaks. Vibe coding gives me a way to do that quickly: build, test, notice what failed, and try again. My portfolio at https://galvinlam.github.io/ shows some of the projects that came out of that loop.
